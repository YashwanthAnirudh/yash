Key Insights

  • The United States reported 32,988,414 new COVID-19 cases and 469,667 new deaths, highlighting the urgent need for localized public health responses in high-impact states.
  • The 85 years and over age group accounted for 311,863 deaths, underscoring the vulnerability of the elderly and the need for targeted healthcare measures.
  • COVID-19 cases surpassed 105 million by mid-2023, reflecting the virus's widespread impact and the importance of continued vaccination and public health strategies.
  • The cumulative death toll reached 1.2 million by 2024, mirroring the severity of case surges and the ongoing necessity of effective healthcare system responses.
  • The daily growth rate of COVID-19 cases fell from 18% in Q2 2020 to near 0% by 2023, indicating the success of mitigation efforts and the development of herd immunity.
  • In 2021, 42% of adults reported symptoms of anxiety and depression, with this figure reducing to 10.8% by late 2023, showing a gradual mental health recovery.
  • Unemployment spiked to 155,420 in 2020, more than doubling compared to 2019, demonstrating the significant economic disruption caused by the pandemic.
  • Restaurant sales dropped to $653 billion in 2020 but rebounded to $1,087 billion in 2023, highlighting the sector's resilience and recovery.
  • Department store sales fell to $113 billion in 2020, with a slight recovery to $133 billion by 2023, reflecting the lasting shift toward e-commerce and changing consumer behavior.
